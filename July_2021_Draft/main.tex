\begin{abstract}
\noindent Nursing home certificate of need (NH-CON) regulations have been both the most restrictive and most common among states with any certificate of need (CON) regulations. We argue that NH-CON is unique among CON regulations because individuals in need of nursing home services can substitute towards services in neighboring states. Using a simple model, we find that the repeal of NH-CON in states that can offer good substitutes will lead to a large increase in total/Medicaid nursing home expenditures, while repealing NH-CON in states that can only offer bad substitutes will lead to a relatively smaller impact on expenditures. We support this prediction empirically by applying a synthetic control analysis of the repeal of NH-CON in Pennsylvania (PA), Indiana (IN), and North Dakota (ND). We find that the repeal of NH-CON in Pennsylvania led to a substantial and significant increase in total nursing home expenditure and Medicaid nursing home expenditure. We also find that the repeal of CON in IN and ND had a small and insignificant impact on total nursing home expenditure and Medicaid nursing home expenditure. These results are consistent with our prediction since PA is likely to offer good nursing home substitutes to large groups of people in neighboring states, while IN and ND are less likely to do that same. Thus, we argue that other states are indirectly pushing some of their Medicaid expenditure to PA.
