\begin{abstract}
\noindent Nursing home certificate of need (NH-CON) regulations have been both the most restrictive and most common among states with any certificate of need (CON) regulations. We argue that NH-CON is unique among CON regulations because individuals in need of nursing home services can substitute towards services in neighboring states. In this paper, we analyze the effect of removing NH-CON regulations  on the quantity of nursing home services and total government and Medicaid nursing home expenditure. Applying a synthetic control analysis of the repeal of NH-CON in Pennsylvania, Indiana, and North Dakota, we find that removing NH-CON regulations causes an increase in the quantity of nursing homes and beds in all three states. We also find that the repeal of NH-CON led to a substantial and significant increase in total government nursing home expenditure and Medicaid nursing home expenditure in Pennsylvania, but not in Indiana or North Dakota. These results are consistent with individuals in neighboring NH-CON states migrating to Pennsylvania after the repeal, where the substitute nursing home services are relatively better than those in Indiana and North Dakota, implying that surrounding states with NH-CON regulations are indirectly pushing some of their Medicaid expenditure to Pennsylvania.
